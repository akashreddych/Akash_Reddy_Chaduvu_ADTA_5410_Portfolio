Introduction

Linear Regression, a cornerstone of statistical modeling, epitomizes my analytical prowess. With two years of hands-on experience, I've honed my skills in employing Linear Regression to unveil relationships within data. At its core, Linear Regression seeks to establish a linear relationship between a dependent variable and one or more independent variables, serving as a powerful tool for prediction and understanding underlying trends.
In my endeavors, I've seamlessly implemented Linear Regression in R, utilizing libraries such as lm() to build models and assess statistical significance. My proficiency extends beyond mere application; I've adeptly interpreted regression coefficients, scrutinized residuals, and validated model assumptions. Moreover, my expertise encompasses both simple and multiple regression scenarios, allowing for the nuanced exploration of complex datasets.
The narrative of my skills in Linear Regression is one of precision, where I leverage statistical insights to inform decision-making. Whether predicting sales trends, understanding the impact of marketing strategies, or uncovering hidden patterns, my adept use of Linear Regression stands as a testament to my ability to extract meaningful insights from data.

The dataset in focus contains the variables 'X' (advertising spend) and 'Y' (monthly sales), sparking the question: Can we discern a correlation between advertising investment and sales figures, and can we leverage this correlation to predict future sales?
